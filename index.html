The Beginning of a Legacy

It started in a small garage in 1989, where a young mechanic named Robert Johnson found his calling. While others saw rust and decay, Robert saw potential and history waiting to be revived.

His first restoration was a 1967 Triumph Bonneville that had been sitting in a barn for fifteen years. Working late nights after his day job, Robert painstakingly brought the machine back to life, sourcing original parts and studying factory blueprints.

When that Bonneville roared to life for the first time, something magical happened. The sound wasn't just an engine startingâ€”it was history breathing again.

The Art of Preservation

Over three decades, we've developed a philosophy that guides every restoration: respect the original while ensuring reliability for the road. We don't just fix motorcyclesâ€”we preserve legacies.

Each machine that enters our workshop receives the same careful attention Robert gave that first Bonneville. Our team of master technicians combines traditional craftsmanship with modern expertise.

We've sourced parts from every corner of the globe, rebuilt engines with the precision of watchmakers, and preserved patina that tells a story no new part ever could.
